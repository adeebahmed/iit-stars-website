For Students

What is Computer Science? What can computers really do? How does technology affect my life?

You may be asking yourself similar questions about computers and technology. This camp will help you answer these questions and help you learn how technology is and can be part of your life.

We want to give you the knowledge to help you match your interests and motivations to a future career.

    Are you an organizer?
    Do you love to be creative?
    Do you like to take things apart?
    Are you always trying to do things better or quicker?
    Do you like to help people?
    Are you interested in computers and robotics?

No matter what kind of background you have with computers we will assist you in learning more about technology, discovering if computer science could be a exciting potential career for you, and finding out how to prepare for a future in technology.

For Parents

We know how important a role you play in encouraging your child’s interests and abilities and introducing her to potential careers. We hope that the following information and tools will be of help to you as you assist your child in exploring computer science.

How do I know if my child might be interested in computer science?

Is your child creative and interested in designing new things? Does she show an interest in her science, math, or technology classes? Does she like to find more than one way to solve a problem? Does she want to find a way to change the world? Maybe your student should consider computer science as a career.

Our camp will expose students to computer science and help them understand the work of technology that is all around them. We engage them with fun activities that challenge them to design creative solutions to common problems found in computer science allowing them to dive into the field and really understand what it is about.

How can I get my kids involved in the camp?

To register your daughter for the camp, check out the camp page and follow the registration link.

Scholarship Request?

Scholarships can be requested for need-based students. Since we do not wish to receive personal financial information from families, we rely on the school to indicate to us if a family has a demonstrated need. Generally, participation in the free and reduced lunch program is an indicator, but a school counselor or other school official may be able to speak to other factors influencing financial need.

Please contact Vida Winans at 312.567.5334 or by email at winans@iit.edu.

For Educators

As educators we know that you have some of the best insight into the interests and aptitudes of your students. We would like to help you identify students who may find great enjoyment and success in the fields of computer science and technology.

How can we help you?

Because you know your students the best we ask that, if you have identified one that may have interests in this field, you recommend our program. We can provide a more in depth education about computer science to help the students better realize how their interests fit in this career.

Outside of the camp, we can also bring demonstrations and activities directly into the classroom during the school year. If you would like to expose your students to the field of computer science and new technology we can work with you to bring in IIT students and professors to show the students what the field is all about. We can also provide you with interesting activities that will engage the students while teaching them to think computationally.

If you are interested in partnering with our program please contact Vida Winans at 312.567.5334 or or by email at winans@iit.edu
