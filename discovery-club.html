About the Club

Throughout the academic year, STARS at the Illinois Institute of Technology work diligently to promote computing to students in our community by means of a Saturday Program

Duration: 5 Saturdays in Fall & Spring

During the Saturday program, we have taught students to program in Scratch, Python, HTML, and even Java. As our students evolve, so does our program. The students have fun and learn to think creatively and program in practical ways.

The Saturday program is offered to Summer camp participants who would like to continue to learn about computers and technology.

Home Activities

Final Project (Mar 5)
Following the worksheet given in class, students should use it to assist them in writing their code for creating their two character game on their webpage. Students can think back to the if-else dice rolling games and plant vs. zombie activity done in the previous weeks in class, and the game-demos in class for inspiration. Students are required to use at least 2 if-statements, 1 if-elseIf statement, and 1 for-loop in their game.

Character-Object's Competition Function (Feb 27)
Students made 2 instances of their character last Saturday in class. Now that there are 2 characters, students will have to create functions for their characters using if-else statements that allow the 2 characters to compete against each other. Students can think back to the if-else dice rolling games and plant vs. zombie activity done in the previous weeks in class for inspiration. Functions must have at least 2 if-statements and at least one if-elseIf statement.

Create Imaginary Character Object (Feb 20)
Students should create an object out of their imaginary character. When creating the object, define the character’s attributes (things/features all “character-object”s will have), the attribute values, and the character’s functions (things that all “character-object”s will do).
(Reminder: For security purposes, no personal pictures or references!)

Imaginary Character Profile Webpage (Feb 13)
Students should make a webpage, using the HTML review as reference, to create a character/avatar. Practice using header, paragraph, break, title, etc. tags to format your webpage similar to the example on the HTML review and what we coded on the projector in class. If students would like to include pictures or sketches on their webpage and do not know how, please bring in the picture(s) on Saturday and we can teach them how. (For security purposes, no personal pictures or references please!)
